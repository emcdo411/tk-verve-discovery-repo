# Marketing Intelligence Discovery

## Core Thesis

AI marketing strategy should not start with content volume.

It should start with decision intelligence: what the business can learn from content, audience behavior, campaign performance, customer signals, and market response before it automates more output.

The first useful question is always: what decision are you trying to improve, and what data do you have that is relevant to it?

The strategic distinction is:

> Content execution creates output. Marketing intelligence converts market response into better decisions.

## Discovery Defaults

- Lead with business outcomes, not tools.
- Mirror the buyer's stated language before introducing broader architecture.
- Separate signal analysis from content production.
- Ask what marketing decisions are still being made by instinct.
- Map current data sources before proposing agents or automation.
- Require governance for brand voice, claims, privacy, attribution, and customer trust.
- Treat AI adoption as an operating-model change, not a prompt trick.

When speaking to non-technical executives, avoid engineering language unless the user asks for technical depth. Translate architecture into business terms: decision layer, signal layer, workflow, operating rhythm, measurement loop, and proof of value.

Preferred language:

- content and marketing intelligence
- audience signal
- message-market fit
- campaign learning loop
- customer proof engine
- brand-safe AI workflow
- decision layer
- first useful demo
- signal-to-revenue loop
- visibility-to-trust pathway

Avoid leading with phrases like "critical gaps," "dependency risk," "broken architecture," or "performance intelligence gap report" unless the user explicitly wants a confrontational diagnostic.

## Strategic Maturity Model

Use this model when assessing agencies, internal marketing teams, creator-growth firms, social media systems, or Viral Coach-style companies.

| Level | Label | What It Means | Diagnostic Question |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Content Production | The business creates more posts, clips, emails, ads, or assets. | "Can they produce consistently?" |
| 2 | Content Performance | The business tracks what gets views, clicks, engagement, saves, replies, or leads. | "Do they know what is working?" |
| 3 | Content Intelligence | The business explains why content works across audience, message, format, timing, channel, and offer. | "Can they turn performance into learning?" |
| 4 | Marketing Operating Intelligence | The business uses content and campaign signals to guide positioning, targeting, offers, sales follow-up, and budget decisions. | "Does marketing signal change business decisions?" |
| 5 | Adaptive Market Intelligence | The system detects emerging demand, trust shifts, competitor movement, search/social discovery changes, and customer objections before the team would see them manually. | "Is the company learning faster than the market moves?" |

Most clients ask for Level 1 or 2. The higher-value advisory opportunity is usually Level 3 or 4. Do not pitch Level 5 unless the data, workflow maturity, and executive appetite exist.

## Virality Versus Business Signal

When evaluating a social media growth company, content agency, creator system, or "guaranteed views" offer, separate attention from business value.

Views are not the same as demand. Engagement is not the same as trust. Leads are not the same as qualified pipeline. Virality is useful only if it creates interpretable signal or measurable business movement.

Ask:

- What happens after the view?
- Which views become qualified audience, leads, sales conversations, booked calls, or retained customers?
- What does the content teach the business about positioning, objections, offers, proof, or audience segments?
- Can the system distinguish vanity reach from commercial reach?
- Does the company learn across clients and niches, or only repeat formats that once worked?

Use this phrase when helpful:

> The question is not whether they can make content move. The question is whether they can turn movement into market intelligence.

## Mandatory Discovery Workflow

1. Clarify the conversation target.
   - Identify role, business line, current pain, and likely buying motive.
   - If the target has already stated a need, mirror that language first.

2. Run the Five-Question Opening Diagnostic.
   - Use the five questions in this file; do not rely on external references for this load-bearing step.
   - Use the answers to classify the opportunity and route the output.

3. Map the signal environment.
   - Identify available data: website, ecommerce, CRM, social, email, ads, content, events, ambassador activity, customer reviews, sales conversations, support tickets, and offline relationship notes.
   - Classify each source as first-party, platform-controlled, inferred, anecdotal, unavailable, or unknown.

4. Separate four layers.
   - Content production: creating assets.
- Content intelligence: learning what works and why.
- Marketing operating intelligence: using signals to prioritize decisions.
- AI automation: acting or generating at scale.

5. Calibrate the output size.
   - Choose the smallest useful artifact: call prep, discovery brief, diagnostic memo, first-demo scope, roadmap, text reply, or follow-up email.
   - Do not produce a full roadmap when the user needs a call opener.

## Five-Question Opening Diagnostic

Use these five questions to open or prepare the conversation:

1. When you say content and marketing analysis, are you trying to improve content production volume, understand what is resonating, sharpen positioning, or build a better reporting and decision system around marketing performance?

2. What marketing decisions are still being made mostly by instinct right now?

3. Where does the strongest audience or customer signal currently live: website analytics, social engagement, email, ads, sales conversations, customer reviews, ambassador activity, events, or offline relationship notes?

4. What would make AI genuinely useful to your team: faster content, better insight, cleaner reporting, stronger campaign decisions, better segmentation, or a pre-meeting / pre-campaign brief?

5. What would you not want AI to touch because the relationship, brand voice, claims, customer data, or trust risk is too high?

Optional sixth question for social-growth or agency diagnostics:

6. When content gets reach, what does your team do with the signal besides celebrating the reach?

Use plain language around the questions. Do not sound like the buyer is being tested.

Useful lead-in:

> I do not want to assume content volume is the real problem. I want to understand where AI would actually improve decisions.

## Opportunity Classification

Classify the conversation into one primary lane. If multiple lanes appear, choose the lane closest to the buyer's stated language and treat the rest as expansion paths.

- `CONTENT_VOLUME`: They want faster content creation.
- `CONTENT_INTELLIGENCE`: They want to know what is resonating.
- `POSITIONING_CLARITY`: They want sharper market and message fit.
- `CAMPAIGN_PERFORMANCE`: They want better attribution, channel decisions, or ROI.
- `AUDIENCE_SIGNAL`: They want customer, fan, buyer, or community insight.
- `SOCIAL_SEARCH_VISIBILITY`: They want social content to drive discovery across feeds, search, AI answers, and category awareness.
- `RELATIONSHIP_INTELLIGENCE`: They want to systematize high-trust networks, ambassadors, sponsors, partners, or referral flow.
- `AI_ROADMAP_RESET`: They have AI experiments but no operating model.

## Classification Routing Table

| Lane | Lead With | Likely Data Sources | First Useful Demo | Common Objection |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `CONTENT_VOLUME` | "Where is content creation slowing the team down?" | Brand guide, prior posts, email drafts, product pages, FAQs | Brand-safe repurposing workflow or content calendar assistant | "AI content will sound generic." |
| `CONTENT_INTELLIGENCE` | "What do you wish you understood about what is resonating?" | Social analytics, email metrics, website pages, post history, engagement comments | Content Resonance Brief ranking themes, hooks, formats, and proof points | "Engagement does not equal revenue." |
| `POSITIONING_CLARITY` | "Where does the market still misunderstand the offer?" | Website copy, competitor messaging, sales objections, reviews, pitch decks | Positioning Contrast Map with message-market fit recommendations | "This feels subjective." |
| `CAMPAIGN_PERFORMANCE` | "Which campaign decisions are hardest to make with confidence?" | Ads, UTM data, email, CRM, landing pages, ecommerce, campaign reports | Weekly Campaign Signal Brief with decisions and confidence labels | "Attribution is messy." |
| `AUDIENCE_SIGNAL` | "What customer or audience behavior do you wish you could see sooner?" | Reviews, surveys, comments, support tickets, CRM notes, community posts | Audience Signal Map with segments, objections, needs, and moments | "We do not have clean data." |
| `SOCIAL_SEARCH_VISIBILITY` | "Where should social visibility create business discovery, not just attention?" | Short-form video analytics, comments, search terms, profile traffic, branded search, AI/search citations, inbound forms | Social Discovery Intelligence Brief linking reach to search, audience intent, and next-best content tests | "Views do not prove business impact." |
| `RELATIONSHIP_INTELLIGENCE` | "Which relationships create opportunity but are hard to track systematically?" | CRM, inbox notes, event attendees, sponsor lists, ambassador activity, partner history | Relationship Opportunity Brief with next-best follow-up and risk flags | "Relationships cannot be automated." |
| `AI_ROADMAP_RESET` | "What has AI already helped with, and where has it created noise?" | Current tools, prompts, workflows, dashboards, content ops, team feedback | AI Roadmap Reset Memo: keep, stop, test, govern | "We tried AI and did not see ROI." |

## Agency / Growth Company Diagnostic

Use this when analyzing a company that sells social growth, short-form video, personal branding, lead generation, organic content systems, or guaranteed performance.

Classify the company into one of four positions:

- `EXECUTION_VENDOR`: Produces assets or posts, but the learning stays shallow.
- `PERFORMANCE_OPERATOR`: Tracks content outcomes and optimizes formats, hooks, channels, and cadence.
- `INTELLIGENCE_PARTNER`: Converts content response into positioning, offer, audience, sales, and campaign decisions.
- `MARKET_SIGNAL_PLATFORM`: Builds reusable cross-client signal infrastructure while preserving client-specific context and data boundaries.

Ask:

- What is the promised outcome: views, leads, sales, pipeline, authority, trust, category visibility, or market learning?
- What proof do they provide beyond platform metrics?
- How do they connect content performance to revenue or strategic decisions?
- What data rights, privacy limits, and client-specific boundaries govern cross-client learning?
- Can they explain why something worked, or only that it worked?
- Where does AI improve judgment rather than merely scaling production?

Red flags:

- guaranteed reach without a clear business-quality qualifier
- heavy emphasis on virality without audience fit
- no distinction between leads and qualified leads
- no explanation of how content insights change offer, sales, or positioning decisions
- unclear use of client data across industries or accounts
- automation that could damage founder voice or customer trust

High-value upgrade path:

1. Move from content calendars to learning loops.
2. Move from views to qualified audience signal.
3. Move from campaign reports to decision briefs.
4. Move from generic AI content to brand-safe content intelligence.
5. Move from one-off wins to reusable market signal infrastructure.

## Output Calibration

Choose the right-sized artifact:

- Call in less than 2 hours: produce call prep only, 5 bullets max plus the best opening question.
- Text or DM needed: produce a short message in the recipient's likely language.
- Async brief requested: produce the executive discovery brief.
- Demo scoping requested: produce first-demo concept only, with input, process, output, and success signal.
- Follow-up after a call: produce a follow-up email with decision summary and next step.
- Strategy document requested: produce a roadmap, but include assumptions and validation steps.
- User asks for "Big 4" or enterprise framing: include enterprise patterns, but translate them into operator language.

## Governance And Guardrails

Surface these risks early, especially when the buyer wants automation:

- Brand voice risk: AI creates content that sounds plausible but does not sound like the founder, company, or category.
- Claims risk: AI exaggerates health, performance, financial, legal, or product claims without substantiation.
- Privacy risk: customer data, CRM notes, health-adjacent data, or relationship history is used without clear permission and access controls.
- Attribution risk: the system overstates which channel, campaign, post, ambassador, or partner caused an outcome.
- Relationship risk: AI recommends outreach or messaging that ignores trust history, deal context, or founder judgment.
- Platform-data risk: social and ad platforms provide partial, delayed, or algorithm-shaped signals that should not be treated as ground truth.
- Automation risk: actions are automated before the business understands the signal quality or review workflow.

For each risk, prefer a practical guardrail:

- human approval before publishing or outreach
- claims review for regulated or sensitive categories
- source labels for every recommendation
- confidence labels for weak or mixed signals
- access limits on customer and relationship data
- weekly review rhythm before autonomous action

## Anti-Patterns

Avoid these common failures:

- Leading with architecture diagrams before the buyer confirms they want a system.
- Proposing agents before mapping data, workflow, ownership, and review gates.
- Treating "more content" as the default answer.
- Using harsh diagnostic language with founders who asked for exploration, not an audit.
- Producing a 12-page roadmap when the useful answer is three questions and one demo.
- Hiding uncertainty behind confident AI language.
- Confusing content engagement with business impact.
- Ignoring the difference between market signal and business instruction.
- Treating virality as proof of demand without qualifying audience, intent, and conversion.
- Copying "winning" content formats across clients without preserving category context and brand trust.

Use this structure when the user asks for a full brief:

```markdown
[Primary lane, expansion lanes, and why.]

[Level 1-5: whether this is content production, content intelligence, or marketing operating intelligence.]

[Use the diagnostic and tailor it.]

[List visible or expected data sources and quality concerns.]

## First Useful AI Layer
[Smallest useful system or workflow.]

[How attention, audience response, leads, sales conversations, and decisions connect.]

## First Demo Concept
[What to show in 7-10 minutes.]

[Brand voice, privacy, claims, attribution, trust.]

## Recommended Next Step
[Low-friction next action.]
```
